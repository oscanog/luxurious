## Lesson 1.1 — What Is Trading?
**Duration:** 5 min

Trading is the act of buying and selling financial assets—stocks, currencies, crypto, or commodities—with the goal of making a profit from price changes.

**Key Concepts:**
- **Buyers (Bulls)** believe the price will go up.
- **Sellers (Bears)** believe the price will go down.
- **Exchanges** are marketplaces where trades happen (e.g., NYSE, Binance).
- Every trade has a buyer AND a seller—someone wins, someone loses.

**Why It Matters:**
Understanding the basic mechanics prevents you from blindly gambling. You're not "playing the market"—you're reading supply and demand.

## Lesson 1.2 — Asset Classes
**Duration:** 6 min

Not all assets are equal. Each class has unique characteristics:

**Crypto** — Digital currencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum. 24/7 markets, high volatility.
**Forex** — Currency pairs (EUR/USD). Largest market in the world, $6T daily volume.
**Stocks** — Ownership in companies (Apple, Tesla). Driven by earnings and news.
**Commodities** — Gold, oil, wheat. Influenced by supply chains and geopolitics.

**Pro Tip:** Start with ONE asset class. Master it before diversifying.

## Lesson 1.3 — Reading a Price Chart
**Duration:** 8 min

Charts are the language of trading. Learn to read them:

**Candlestick Anatomy:**
- **Open** — Where price started
- **Close** — Where price ended
- **High/Low** — Maximum and minimum during the period
- **Green candle** = Close > Open (bullish)
- **Red candle** = Close < Open (bearish)

**Timeframes:**
- 1m, 5m, 15m = Scalping/Day trading
- 1H, 4H = Swing trading
- 1D, 1W = Position trading

Look at the chart on your dashboard—each bar is a candlestick!

## Lesson 1.4 — Order Types
**Duration:** 6 min

How you enter and exit matters:

**Market Order** — Buy/sell NOW at current price. Fast but may slip.
**Limit Order** — Buy/sell at YOUR price. Patient but might not fill.
**Stop Order** — Triggers when price hits a level. Used for protection.
**Stop-Limit** — Combo: triggers like a stop, executes like a limit.

**Golden Rule:** Never enter a trade without knowing your exit.

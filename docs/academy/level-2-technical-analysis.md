## Lesson 2.1 — Support & Resistance
**Duration:** 7 min

Price doesn't move randomly. It respects invisible walls:

**Support** — A price level where buying pressure stops the fall. Think of it as a floor.
**Resistance** — A price level where selling pressure stops the rise. Think of it as a ceiling.

**How to find them:**
- Look for areas where price bounced multiple times
- Round numbers often act as S/R (e.g., $60,000 for BTC)
- When support breaks, it becomes resistance (and vice versa)

This single concept is the foundation of all technical analysis.

## Lesson 2.2 — Trend Lines & Channels
**Duration:** 6 min

The trend is your friend—until it ends.

**Uptrend** — Higher highs + higher lows. Draw line connecting lows.
**Downtrend** — Lower highs + lower lows. Draw line connecting highs.
**Channel** — Two parallel trend lines containing price.

**Trading Rules:**
- Buy at the bottom of an uptrend channel
- Sell at the top of a downtrend channel
- A break of the channel = potential reversal

## Lesson 2.3 — Moving Averages
**Duration:** 7 min

Smoothing out the noise:

**SMA (Simple Moving Average)** — Average of last N candles. Equal weight.
**EMA (Exponential Moving Average)** — More weight on recent prices. Faster reaction.

**Key MAs to watch:**
- 20 EMA = Short-term trend
- 50 SMA = Medium-term trend
- 200 SMA = Long-term trend (institutional favorite)

**Golden Cross** = 50 MA crosses above 200 MA → Bullish
**Death Cross** = 50 MA crosses below 200 MA → Bearish

---

## Lesson 2.4 — Volume Analysis
**Duration:** 5 min

Price tells you WHAT happened. Volume tells you WHO was behind it.

- **High volume + price up** = Strong buying (real move)
- **Low volume + price up** = Weak buying (likely to reverse)
- **Volume spike at support** = Institutions stepping in

**Rule:** Never trust a breakout on low volume.

---

## Lesson 2.5 — Chart Patterns
**Duration:** 8 min

Patterns repeat because human psychology repeats:

**Reversal Patterns:**
- Double Top/Bottom — M and W shapes
- Head & Shoulders — Three peaks, middle is highest

**Continuation Patterns:**
- Flags & Pennants — Brief pause before trend continues
- Triangles — Ascending, Descending, Symmetrical

**Pro Tip:** Patterns work best on higher timeframes (4H, 1D).
